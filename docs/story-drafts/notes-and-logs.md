## Humble Abode

### One's original note
*Found late-game: Tev keeps it folded in a tin. He shows it only if the player
asks him "how many?" (see Tev dialogue addition below). Human handwriting —
the player has been reading Tev's COPY of this since minute one of the game.*

> Drink from the bank where the water runs, not where it sits.
> Fish when the light is low. Cook everything.
> Sleep before you think you need to.
>
> Routine is a rope. Hold it.
>
> — later, other ink, shakier: —
> If another one falls: give them the rope.

### One's grave marker
*By the fishing bank. Weathered flat stone, alien script above, human
letters scratched beneath. No interaction prompt until after the Interview
names the owners; then it becomes a `Discoverable`.*

> HOLD THE ROPE.

### Tev — "how many?" dialogue addition
*One new branch in TevDialogue, unlocked post-Interview.*

- **Player:** How many of us have you taken in, Tev?
- **Tev:** ...Eight. You're eight.
- **Tev:** The first one taught me the routine. The water, the fish, the fire. I only wrote it down.
- **Tev:** Don't tell me how it ends this time. I find I do better work when I don't know.

### Alien3's cassette drawer
*Prop label / examine text. Visible behind his counter from day one.*

> A drawer of cassette tapes, kept carefully. Eight of them.
> One slot is empty.

### The rebel note (concert crowd handoff)
*Given by a dancer mid-set at the active stage. Triggers A2-6.*

> You dance like someone who hasn't been told.
> North stage. Next dark. Bring nothing that watches.
> — P.

### Guitar shop note
*Pinned behind the counter.*

> The melody everyone asks about came from the first offworlder.
> He hummed it wrong all year and we learned the wrong version.
> It's ours now. That's how songs work.

## Constant Companion (MoonBase) — the Keeper's notes (Four)

### Note 1 — galley table
> Resupply's late again. Radioed the Abode, got a clerk who kept asking
> whether I'd "observed anything irregular."
> Irregular is a moon, friend. Everything here is irregular.
> Sent him my grocery list. He logged it as an incident.

### Note 2 — bunk, under the pillow
> Third night running, the Eye reads brighter at zenith. So I charted it
> against my own logs, and here's the thing — it is NOT brighter at zenith.
> It's brighter WHEN I CHART IT.
> Chart away: up she comes. Chart down: steady as stone.
> I have stopped charting. I have not stopped knowing that it knows the
> difference.

### Note 3 — inside the airlock, last entry
> Orders came through: cease resupply requests, seal the station, await
> retrieval. No retrieval is coming, log, and we both know it.
> Here's what nobody at the Accord will put in writing: it isn't getting
> brighter. It's getting closer to the glass.
> I'm going out to look at it properly. A thing that watches you through a
> window is a coward until proven otherwise.
> — K.

### The Keeper's suit
*Far side of the moon, standing upright, helmet tilted toward Watchful Eye.
`Discoverable`, no marker, no prompt beyond "Examine."*

> The suit stands on its own. It has stood here a long time.
> The helmet is tilted up, and to the left — exactly where the Eye
> would have been, twelve years ago tonight.

## Fiery Twin — consortium camp logs (with Two)

### Log 1 — site office
> Claims 7 through 12 tagged and lodged. The human — owner's mark "II" —
> works the terminator line like she was born to heat. Doesn't say where
> she's from. Company policy is not to ask, and for once everyone follows
> policy.

### Log 2 — survey tent
> Anomaly, boreholes 3, 5 and 9: soundings deflect the same direction.
> Not toward ore. Down and inward, past the crust, there's a — the
> geologist keeps calling it a "regard." A regard.
> I asked him to use engineering words. He said those were the
> engineering words.

### Log 3 — site manager's desk, final entry
*Orderly handwriting. The camp around it: meals half-eaten, tools racked,
beds made.*

> We have discussed it and we are going to go and see. All of us.
> The equipment stays. Equipment can't be received.
> If anyone reads this: we were not taken. We were invited, we checked the
> maths, and the maths was polite.
> The door is on the other side of the crust. Ours is. Yours will be
> wherever yours is.
> — Site Manager, final entry, all accounts settled.

*("Received" — the same word HAL reads off the plaque at Watchful Eye,
nineteen years later. Nobody comments on it. The player either catches it
or doesn't.)*

## Tumbling Bean — Five's cassette (found in wreck cockpit)

*Audio. Wind, engine hum, calm professional voice.*

> Approach vector logged. Storm's stable. Always stable — that's the tell,
> friends: storms don't hold station for two hundred years.

circles. Duration of contact: six years, four months."*

## Six's cassette — "The Trade Back"
*The tape the player traded away, unheard, in the first hour. Audio: bank
sounds, morning birds, water.*

> Eighth recording. Last one, I think.
> I taught the kid at the guitar shop the rest of the song. It was my
> mother's. Now it's theirs — that's how songs work, HAL says.
> [pause]
> If you're hearing this, the drawer gave you up. Which means you traded it
> away without listening — don't feel bad. I did the same with the one I
> inherited. There's a chain of us, tape to tape, and nobody's ever heard
> the one they carried. Maybe that's the only private thing in this whole
> system.
> [pause]
> Coordinates on side B. Don't be afraid of the door.
> Be afraid of staying. Staying is how you end up a note in a drawer.
> — Six.
